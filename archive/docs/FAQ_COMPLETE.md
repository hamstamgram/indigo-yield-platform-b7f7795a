## 1. Account & Verification

### Q: How do I create an account?

**A**: Creating an account is simple:
1. Visit the Indigo Yield Platform website
2. Click "Sign Up" or "Create Account"
3. Enter your email address and create a password
4. Verify your email by clicking the link sent to your inbox
5. Complete the onboarding wizard with your personal information

You'll need to complete KYC (Know Your Customer) verification before you can start investing.

### Q: What is KYC and why is it required?

**A**: KYC stands for "Know Your Customer." It's a regulatory requirement that helps prevent fraud, money laundering, and other financial crimes.

**Required Documents**:
- Government-issued photo ID (passport, driver's license)
- Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement less than 3 months old)
- Accredited investor verification (if applicable)

This is a legal requirement for all investment platforms.

### Q: How long does account verification take?

**A**: Typical timeline:
- **Email Verification**: Instant (check spam folder if not received)
- **KYC Document Review**: 1-3 business days
- **Accredited Investor Verification**: 2-5 business days
- **Total**: Usually 3-7 business days for full approval

You'll receive email updates as your application progresses.

### Q: What does "Pending" account status mean?

**A**: "Pending" means your account is being reviewed. During this time:
- ✓ You can log in and explore the platform
- ✓ You can view educational materials
- ✗ You cannot make deposits or invest
- ✗ You cannot request withdrawals

Once approved, your status changes to "Active" and full access is granted.

### Q: I haven't heard back about my application. What should I do?

**A**: If you haven't received an update after 5 business days:
1. Check your spam/junk folder for emails
2. Log in to check your account status
3. Contact support at support@indigoyield.com (example)
4. Provide your application email and date submitted

### Q: Can I have multiple accounts?

**A**: No, each individual or entity may only have one account. Multiple accounts violate our terms of service and may result in account suspension.

If you need to invest on behalf of multiple entities, contact support about entity account options.

### Q: How do I close my account?

**A**: To close your account:
1. Withdraw all funds (no balance remaining)
2. Contact support requesting account closure
3. Confirm you want to proceed
4. Account will be closed within 5 business days

**Important**: This action is permanent and cannot be undone. You'll need to create a new account to invest again.

### Q: What if I change my name (marriage, legal change)?

**A**: Legal name changes require documentation:
1. Contact support
2. Provide legal proof (marriage certificate, court order)
3. Submit updated ID documents
4. Admin will verify and update
5. Allow 3-5 business days

### Q: Do I need to be an accredited investor?

**A**: Requirements vary by fund:
- Some funds open to all investors
- Some funds require accredited investor status
- Check specific fund requirements before applying

**Accredited Investor Criteria** (US):
- Individual income >$200,000/year (last 2 years)
- Joint income >$300,000/year (last 2 years)
- Net worth >$1,000,000 (excluding primary residence)
- Professional certifications (Series 7, 65, 82 licenses)

## 2. Deposits & Funding

### Q: What is the minimum investment?

**A**: Minimum investment varies by fund:
- Typically ranges from $1,000 to $100,000
- Check fund details for specific minimum
- Some funds have higher minimums for institutional investors

### Q: How do I make a deposit?

**A**: Deposit process:
1. Contact your fund administrator for instructions
2. Receive secure deposit details via email
3. Initiate transfer from your bank or exchange
4. Include required reference number
5. Notify platform with confirmation

**Methods Accepted**:
- ACH transfer (US investors)
- Wire transfer (domestic and international)
- Cryptocurrency transfer (specific funds)

### Q: How long do deposits take to process?

**A**: Processing times vary by method:
- **ACH**: 3-5 business days
- **Wire Transfer**: 1-2 business days
- **Cryptocurrency**: Hours to 1 business day (depending on network)

Your dashboard will show "Pending" until deposit is confirmed.

### Q: I made a deposit but don't see it in my account. What's wrong?

**A**: Possible reasons:
1. **Still Processing**: Check processing timeline for your method
2. **Missing Reference**: Deposit may need manual matching
3. **Wrong Amount**: Minimum not met
4. **Bank Delay**: Holidays or weekends

**Steps to Resolve**:
- Wait full processing timeline before worrying
- Check your transaction history for "Pending" deposit
- Email support with transaction confirmation if after 5 business days
- Provide: date, amount, reference number, confirmation screenshot

### Q: Can I deposit more than the minimum?

**A**: Yes, you can deposit any amount above the minimum. Some funds may have maximum limits for single deposits. Check fund terms or contact support for large deposits.

### Q: Are there deposit fees?

**A**: Deposit fee policies:
- **Platform**: Typically no fee charged by platform
- **Bank/Exchange**: Your bank may charge wire or transfer fees
- **Cryptocurrency**: Network gas fees apply

Check with your financial institution for their fee schedule.

### Q: Can I deposit from a joint account?

**A**: Deposits from joint accounts require:
- Account registered in same name(s) as joint account
- Documentation showing joint ownership
- Approval from compliance team

Contact support before depositing from joint accounts.

### Q: What if I accidentally deposit the wrong amount?

**A**: If you deposit the wrong amount:
1. Contact support immediately
2. Provide transaction details
3. **Too Little**: May need to deposit additional to meet minimum
4. **Too Much**: Can withdraw excess or leave invested

Do not attempt to "fix" with another transfer before contacting support.

## 3. Withdrawals

### Q: How do I request a withdrawal?

**A**: Withdrawal process:
1. Log in and go to "Withdrawals"
2. Click "New Withdrawal"
3. Select fund to withdraw from
4. Enter amount
5. Choose partial or full withdrawal
6. Submit request
7. Wait for admin approval and processing

See Quick Reference Guide for step-by-step checklist.

### Q: How long do withdrawals take?

**A**: Total timeline typically 5-7 business days:
- **Admin Review**: 1-2 business days
- **Processing**: 1-3 business days
- **Settlement**: 1-2 business days

**Factors That May Delay**:
- Lock-up period not met
- Missing bank information
- Compliance review required
- Holidays or weekends

### Q: Is there a minimum withdrawal amount?

**A**: Minimum withdrawal varies by fund:
- Typically $1,000 - $5,000 minimum
- Full withdrawals exempt from minimum
- Check fund terms for specific amount

### Q: Can I cancel a withdrawal request?

**A**: Yes, IF not yet approved:
- **Pending Status**: Can cancel anytime
- **Approved Status**: Cannot cancel (processing started)
- **Processing/Completed**: Too late to cancel

To cancel: Go to withdrawal request and click "Cancel Request"

### Q: What is a lock-up period?

**A**: A lock-up period is the minimum time you must keep funds invested before withdrawing.

**Common Lock-Up Periods**:
- 30 days (short-term funds)
- 90 days (medium-term funds)
- 6-12 months (long-term funds)

Lock-up starts from your deposit date. Check fund terms for specific period.

### Q: My withdrawal was rejected. Why?

**A**: Common rejection reasons:
1. **Lock-up Not Met**: Too early to withdraw
2. **Insufficient Balance**: Requested more than available
3. **Missing Bank Info**: Need to add payment details
4. **Compliance Hold**: Account requires review

**Solution**: Check rejection reason in withdrawal details and address the issue noted.

### Q: Where do withdrawn funds go?

**A**: Funds sent to:
- Bank account on file (ACH/wire)
- Cryptocurrency wallet address provided
- Method used for original deposit (if applicable)

Verify your payment information is correct before requesting withdrawal.

### Q: Can I make partial withdrawals?

**A**: Yes, most funds allow partial withdrawals if:
- ✓ Amount meets minimum withdrawal
- ✓ Remaining balance meets minimum investment
- ✓ Lock-up period satisfied

Example: $100,000 invested, can withdraw $50,000 if both amounts meet minimums.

### Q: Are there withdrawal fees?

**A**: Withdrawal fees vary:
- Some funds charge no withdrawal fees
- Some charge flat fee ($25-$100)
- Some charge percentage fee (1-2%)
- Early withdrawal may incur penalty

Check fund fee schedule for specific details.

## 4. Fees & Charges

### Q: What fees do I pay?

**A**: Common fees:
1. **Management Fee**: Annual fee (typically 1-2% of AUM)
   - Charged monthly or quarterly
   - Based on average account value

2. **Performance Fee**: Share of profits (typically 10-20%)
   - Only charged if fund profits above benchmark
   - Subject to high water mark

3. **Withdrawal Fee**: Per withdrawal (if applicable)
   - Flat fee or percentage
   - May be waived for long-term investors

**No Hidden Fees**: All fees disclosed in fund documents.

### Q: How are fees calculated?

**A**: **Management Fee Example**:
```
Annual rate: 2%
Average monthly balance: $50,000
Monthly fee = $50,000 × 2% ÷ 12 = $83.33
```

**Performance Fee Example**:
```
Beginning value: $100,000
Ending value: $115,000
Gain: $15,000
Benchmark return: 8% ($8,000)
Excess return: $7,000
Performance fee (20%): $7,000 × 20% = $1,400
```

### Q: When are fees charged?

**A**: Fee schedule:
- **Management Fees**: Monthly or quarterly (see fund terms)
- **Performance Fees**: Annually (end of year)
- **Withdrawal Fees**: At time of withdrawal

Fees automatically deducted from your account balance.

### Q: How do I see what fees I've paid?

**A**: View fees in multiple places:
1. **Monthly Statement**: Fee section (Page 5)
2. **Transaction History**: Filter by "Fee" type
3. **Tax Reports**: Annual fee summary
4. **Analytics**: Fee breakdown charts

### Q: Can fees be waived or reduced?

**A**: Fee waivers/reductions may be available for:
- Large investors (>$1M typically)
- Promotional periods
- Special arrangements

Contact your account manager to discuss.

### Q: Are fees tax-deductible?

**A**: Investment management fees MAY be deductible:
- Consult tax professional
- Rules vary by investor type (individual, entity, IRA)
- Subject to itemization requirements
- Keep all fee records

**Important**: This is not tax advice. Consult CPA or tax advisor.

## 9. Investments & Performance

### Q: What returns can I expect?

**A**: Important disclaimers:
- Past performance does not guarantee future results
- Returns vary by fund and market conditions
- No guaranteed returns
- Investment involves risk of loss

**Typical Range** (historical, not guaranteed):
- Conservative funds: 3-6% annually
- Moderate funds: 6-12% annually
- Aggressive funds: 12-20% annually (with higher risk)

Review fund prospectus for historical performance.

### Q: How is my return calculated?

**A**: Return calculation:
```
Return = (Ending Value - Beginning Value - Contributions + Withdrawals) / Beginning Value

Example:
Beginning: $100,000
Ending: $112,000
Contributions: $10,000
Withdrawals: $5,000
Net Gain: $112,000 - $100,000 - $10,000 + $5,000 = $7,000
Return: $7,000 / $100,000 = 7%
```

Time-weighted return accounts for deposits/withdrawals timing.

### Q: What is NAV (Net Asset Value)?

**A**: NAV is the per-share price of the fund:
- Calculated daily
- Total fund assets minus liabilities divided by shares
- Your share price for purchases and redemptions
- Changes daily based on underlying holdings

**Example**:
- Fund assets: $10 million
- Fund liabilities: $100,000
- Shares outstanding: 100,000
- NAV = ($10M - $100K) / 100K = $99 per share

### Q: How often are returns calculated?

**A**: Performance reporting:
- **Daily**: NAV updated daily
- **Monthly**: Monthly return in statement
- **Quarterly**: Quarterly performance summary
- **Annually**: Annual return report

All cumulative (year-to-date, since inception) available in dashboard.

### Q: Can I change my investment allocation?

**A**: Yes, you can:
1. Request withdrawal from Fund A
2. Request deposit to Fund B
3. Or ask about internal transfer

**Fee Considerations**:
- Withdrawal fees may apply

### Q: What happens to dividends or yield?

**A**: Yield distribution:
- Automatically reinvested (default)
- Or paid out as cash (if elected)

Check fund terms for distribution frequency (daily, monthly, quarterly).

### Q: How do I compare fund performance?

**A**: Compare funds:
1. Analytics → Fund Comparison
2. Select funds to compare
3. View side-by-side:
   - Returns by period
   - Volatility metrics
   - Fee impact
   - Risk-adjusted returns

Or review individual fund performance pages.

### Q: What is a high water mark?

**A**: High water mark for performance fees:
- Highest NAV ever reached
- Performance fee only charged on gains above this level
- Protects you from paying fees twice on same gains

**Example**:
- Previous high: $110/share
- Current: $105/share
- Recover to $111/share
- Performance fee only on $1 gain (not $6)

## 10. Tax & Compliance

### Q: What tax forms will I receive?

**A**: Tax forms vary by investor type and fund structure:

**Common Forms**:
- **Form 1099**: Interest, dividends, gains/losses
- **Schedule K-1**: Partnership income (certain funds)
- **Form 1042-S**: Foreign investors
- **Summary Statement**: Annual tax summary

**Delivery**: By January 31st (1099) or March 15th (K-1).

### Q: When do I receive tax forms?

**A**: Tax form schedule:
- **1099 Forms**: By January 31st
- **K-1 Forms**: By March 15th
- **Corrected Forms**: If needed, by mid-March

**Delivery Method**:
- Email notification when available
- Download from Documents → Tax Forms
- Physical mail if elected

### Q: Are my investments taxable?

**A**: Tax treatment depends on:
- **Account Type**: Individual, IRA, entity
- **Transaction Type**: Gains, income, distributions
- **Holding Period**: Short-term vs. long-term
- **Investor Status**: US person, foreign investor

**General Guidelines** (consult tax advisor):
- Interest/dividends: Ordinary income
- Short-term gains (<1 year): Ordinary income
- Long-term gains (>1 year): Capital gains rates
- IRA: Tax-deferred until withdrawal

**Important**: This is not tax advice. Consult CPA or tax professional.

### Q: Can I invest through an IRA or retirement account?

**A**: IRA investments:
- Some funds accept IRA investments
- Requires IRA custodian relationship
- May have higher minimums
- Special procedures apply

Contact support to discuss IRA investment options.

### Q: How do I report my investments on tax returns?

**A**: Tax reporting steps:
1. Receive tax forms from platform (1099, K-1)
2. Download annual tax report from platform
3. Provide all forms to tax preparer
4. Report income/gains per forms
5. Consider deductibility of fees

**Do NOT use platform reports as official forms** - use IRS forms only.

### Q: What is my cost basis?

**A**: Cost basis tracking:
- Platform tracks cost basis for each position
- Shows on statements and tax reports
- Important for calculating gains/losses
- May be average cost or specific identification

**View Cost Basis**:
- Account → Positions → Click position → View cost basis

### Q: Do I need to report if I didn't sell anything?

**A**: Yes, still report:
- Interest and dividends are taxable even without sales
- Received Form 1099 or K-1? Must report
- Consult tax advisor if uncertain

### Q: How long should I keep tax records?

**A**: Recommended retention:
- **Tax Returns**: 7 years minimum
- **Investment Statements**: 7 years
- **Trade Confirmations**: 7 years
- **Tax Forms (1099, K-1)**: Permanent
- **Cost Basis Records**: Until investment sold + 7 years

**Reason**: IRS can audit returns up to 7 years in some cases.

### Q: I'm not a US citizen. How does that affect my taxes?

**A**: Foreign investor considerations:
- May be subject to withholding tax (typically 30%)
- May qualify for reduced rate under tax treaty
- Receive Form 1042-S (not 1099)
- Additional reporting requirements

**W-8BEN Form**: Foreign investors must complete for tax treaty benefits.

Consult international tax specialist for guidance.
