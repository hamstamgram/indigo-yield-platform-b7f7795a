## 1. Introduction

### Welcome to Indigo Yield Platform

Indigo Yield Platform is a professional investment platform designed to help you manage your cryptocurrency investments with yield-generating strategies. This guide will help you navigate all features and make the most of your investment experience.

### What You Can Do

- **Track Your Portfolio**: View your complete investment holdings in real-time
- **Manage Transactions**: Make deposits and request withdrawals
- **Access Statements**: Download monthly investment statements
- **Generate Reports**: Create tax reports and performance analytics
- **Secure Your Account**: Manage security settings and sessions
- **Get Support**: Access help resources and submit support tickets

### Who Should Use This Guide

This guide is designed for:
- **New Investors**: Setting up their first account
- **Active Investors**: Managing existing investments
- **All Users**: Learning about platform features and best practices

## 2. Getting Started

### 2.1 Creating Your Account

#### Step 1: Visit the Platform
1. Open your web browser and navigate to the Indigo Yield Platform login page
2. Look for the "Sign Up" or "Create Account" option

#### Step 2: Provide Your Email
1. Enter your email address (this will be your username)
2. Create a strong password:
   - At least 8 characters
   - Include uppercase and lowercase letters
   - Include at least one number
   - Include at least one special character

#### Step 3: Verify Your Email
1. Check your email inbox for a verification message
2. Click the verification link in the email
3. This confirms your email address is valid

**What Happens Next**: After email verification, you'll be directed to complete your profile through the onboarding wizard.

---

### 2.2 Onboarding Wizard

The onboarding wizard guides you through three essential steps to set up your account.

#### Step 1: Profile Setup

**What You'll Need**:
- Legal first and last name
- Phone number
- Mailing address (if required)

**How to Complete**:
1. When you first log in, you'll see the onboarding wizard
2. The progress bar at the top shows: Profile Setup → Document Review → Fund Selection
3. Fill in all required fields marked with a red asterisk (*)
4. Click "Continue" to move to the next step

**Tips**:
- Use your legal name exactly as it appears on government ID
- Your phone number should include country code (e.g., +1 for US)
- Double-check all information before continuing

#### Step 2: Document Review

**What You'll Do**:
1. Review the fund terms and conditions
2. Read the KYC (Know Your Customer) requirements
3. Review investment disclosures and risk factors
4. Acknowledge that you've read and understood all documents

**Documents You'll See**:
- Fund Subscription Agreement
- Privacy Policy
- Terms of Service
- Risk Disclosure Statement
- KYC Requirements

**How to Proceed**:
1. Read each document carefully
2. Check the box to acknowledge you've reviewed the documents
3. You can download copies for your records
4. Click "I Acknowledge" to continue

**Important**: You must acknowledge all documents to proceed. Take your time to understand the terms.

#### Step 3: Fund Selection

**What You'll Do**:
Select the yield-generating funds you want to invest in.

**Available Fund Information**:
Each fund displays:
- Fund name and code
- Yield strategy description
- Historical performance (if applicable)
- Minimum investment amount
- Risk level

**How to Select Funds**:
1. Browse available funds
2. Read the description and strategy for each fund
3. Check boxes next to funds you're interested in
4. You can select multiple funds
5. Click "Complete Setup" when finished

**What Happens Next**: After completing the onboarding wizard, your profile status changes to "Active" and you can access the full platform dashboard.

## 4. Making Deposits

### 4.1 Deposit Methods Overview

The platform supports multiple deposit methods:
- **ACH Transfer**: Bank transfers (US investors)
- **Wire Transfer**: Domestic and international
- **Cryptocurrency Transfer**: Direct blockchain transfers

**Note**: Actual deposit functionality may vary. Contact your fund administrator for specific deposit instructions.

### 4.2 Important Deposit Information

#### Minimum Deposit Amounts
Each fund has minimum investment requirements:
- Check fund details before initiating deposit
- Minimum amounts typically range from $1,000 to $100,000

#### Processing Times
- **ACH**: 3-5 business days
- **Wire**: 1-2 business days
- **Crypto**: Varies by network (minutes to hours for confirmations)

#### What You'll Need
- **For ACH/Wire**: Bank account information, routing numbers
- **For Crypto**: Wallet address, network selection (ERC-20, native, etc.)

### 4.3 Deposit Best Practices

**Before You Deposit**:
1. Verify minimum investment amounts
2. Ensure KYC verification is complete
3. Double-check bank or wallet addresses
4. Keep transaction receipts

**After You Deposit**:
1. You'll receive email confirmation
2. Deposit appears as "Pending" in your transaction history
3. Once confirmed, balance updates in your portfolio
4. You may need to wait for full settlement before withdrawing

**Tips for Success**:
- Always use the exact deposit instructions provided
- Include required reference numbers or memo fields
- For crypto: Send a small test transaction first
- Save all confirmation numbers

- **Status Badge**: Current state of transaction
- **Date & Time**: When transaction was created

### 5.4 Transaction Status Badges

## 6. Managing Withdrawals

### 6.1 Withdrawal Request Process

Access via: **Navigation Menu → Withdrawals**

### 6.2 Creating a Withdrawal Request

#### Step 1: Click "New Withdrawal"
Located in the top-right corner of the Withdrawals page.

#### Step 2: Select Fund
**What You'll See**:
- Dropdown list of your fund positions
- Available balance for each fund shown

**How to Choose**:
1. Click the dropdown menu
2. Review available balances
3. Select the fund you want to withdraw from

**Example Display**:
```
Strategic Crypto Fund - $50,000.00 available
```

#### Step 3: Enter Withdrawal Amount

**What to Know**:
- Enter amount in USD
- Cannot exceed available balance
- Must meet minimum withdrawal amounts (if applicable)

**Tips**:
- The form shows your maximum withdrawal amount
- If you enter too much, you'll see an error message
- Use the decimal point for cents (e.g., 5000.50)

#### Step 4: Choose Withdrawal Type

**Options**:
- **Partial Withdrawal**: Withdraw some funds, keep position open
- **Full Withdrawal**: Close position completely

**When to Use Each**:
- Partial: You want to take some profit but stay invested
- Full: You want to completely exit the investment

#### Step 5: Add Notes (Optional)

**What to Include**:
- Special delivery instructions
- Preferred payment method
- Urgency of request
- Any questions for administrators

**Example Notes**:
```
Please process via wire transfer to Bank of America
account ending in 1234. Needed by end of month for tax
planning.
```

#### Step 6: Submit Request

1. Review all information carefully
2. Click "Submit Request"
3. You'll see a confirmation message
4. Request appears in your withdrawal history

### 6.3 Withdrawal Request Statuses

After submission, your request moves through several status stages:

#### Pending
- **What It Means**: Request received, awaiting review
- **What Happens**: Administrative team reviews your request
- **Typical Timeline**: 1-3 business days

#### Approved
- **What It Means**: Request approved by administrator
- **What Happens**: Processing begins
- **Your Action**: None needed, wait for processing

#### Processing
- **What It Means**: Funds are being transferred
- **What Happens**: Bank transfer or crypto transaction initiated
- **Typical Timeline**: 1-5 business days depending on method

#### Completed
- **What It Means**: Withdrawal successful
- **What Happens**: Funds sent to your designated account
- **What You'll See**: Updated portfolio balance

#### Rejected
- **What It Means**: Request could not be approved
- **What Happens**: You'll see rejection reason
- **Your Action**: Review reason, address issues, resubmit if applicable

**Common Rejection Reasons**:
- Insufficient balance
- Lock-up period not met
- KYC verification required
- Missing bank information

#### Cancelled
- **What It Means**: Request was cancelled
- **Who Can Cancel**: You (before approval) or admin
- **What Happens**: Request removed from queue

### 6.4 Viewing Withdrawal History

**Each Withdrawal Shows**:
- **Amount Requested**: Original withdrawal amount
- **Fund Name**: Which investment the withdrawal is from
- **Type**: Partial or Full withdrawal
- **Status Badge**: Current status with color coding
- **Date Created**: When you submitted the request
- **Approved Amount**: May differ from requested if partially approved
- **Your Notes**: Any notes you added
- **Admin Notes**: Messages from the administrative team

**Special Indicators**:

**⚠️ Rejection Notice** (Red Box):
```
Rejected: Insufficient cleared funds. Please wait for
pending deposit to settle before requesting withdrawal.
```

**ℹ️ Admin Notes** (Gray Box):
```
Admin notes: Processed via wire transfer on 11/15.
Reference number: WT-2024-11234.
```

### 6.5 Withdrawal Best Practices

**Before Requesting Withdrawal**:
1. Check available balance (not just total value)
2. Review any lock-up periods or withdrawal restrictions
3. Verify your bank/wallet information is up to date
4. Consider tax implications
5. Check for pending deposits that haven't settled

**Timing Considerations**:
- Allow 5-7 business days for complete processing
- Request early in the month for month-end needs
- Avoid holiday periods if time-sensitive

**Communication Tips**:
- Use the Notes field for special instructions
- Include preferred contact method for questions
- Provide alternative contact info if traveling

## 7. Documents Center

### 7.1 Accessing Your Documents

Access via: **Navigation Menu → Documents**

The Documents Center is your secure repository for all investment-related documents.

### 7.2 Types of Documents

#### Monthly Statements
- **What They Are**: Official monthly investment reports
- **What They Include**:
  - Beginning and ending balances
  - All transactions for the month
  - Performance summary
  - Fee breakdown
  - Asset allocation
- **When Available**: Generated on the 5th of each month for previous month
- **Format**: PDF, downloadable and printable

#### Tax Forms
- **What They Are**: Annual tax reporting documents
- **Common Forms**:
  - Form 1099 (for US investors)
  - K-1 (for partnership investments)
  - Foreign tax documents (if applicable)
- **When Available**: By January 31st for prior tax year
- **Important**: Keep these for your tax records

#### Fund Documents
- **Prospectus**: Detailed fund information
- **Subscription Agreement**: Your signed investment contract
- **Operating Agreement**: Fund governance documents
- **Amendment Notices**: Any changes to fund terms

#### Compliance Documents
- **KYC Documents**: Know Your Customer verification
- **Accredited Investor Verification**: Proof of investment eligibility
- **Identity Verification**: Copies of submitted ID documents

#### Correspondence
- **Welcome Letters**: Initial account setup confirmations
- **Annual Reports**: Yearly fund performance and outlook
- **Important Notices**: Time-sensitive communications

### 7.3 How to Download Documents

**Method 1: Individual Download**
1. Navigate to Documents page
2. Find the document you need
3. Click the download icon (↓) next to the document
4. File downloads to your device
5. Save in a secure location

**Method 2: Bulk Download**
1. Check boxes next to multiple documents
2. Click "Download Selected" button
3. Documents download as a ZIP file

### 7.4 Viewing Documents

**Online Viewing**:
1. Click document name (not download icon)
2. Document opens in browser viewer
3. Use browser zoom for readability
4. Print directly from viewer if needed

**Mobile Viewing**:
- Documents open in device PDF viewer
- Can share to other apps for storage
- Recommend saving to secure cloud storage

### 7.5 Document Organization Tips

**Create a Filing System**:
```
My Indigo Documents/
├── 2024/
│   ├── Monthly Statements/
│   ├── Tax Forms/
│   └── Correspondence/
├── 2025/
│   ├── Monthly Statements/
│   ├── Tax Forms/
│   └── Correspondence/

    ├── Initial Agreements/
    └── Amendments/
```

**Best Practices**:
- Download statements monthly (don't wait until year-end)
- Keep tax documents for at least 7 years
- Back up important documents to cloud storage
- Keep physical copies of signed agreements

### 7.6 Document Security

**Platform Security**:
- All documents encrypted during storage and transmission
- Access limited to account owner only
- Audit trail of who accessed what and when
- Automatic logout after inactivity

**Your Responsibility**:
- Don't share login credentials
- Log out when using shared computers
- Use secure networks (avoid public WiFi for downloads)
- Password-protect downloaded files if storing locally

### 9.3 Security Settings

Access via: **Account → Security Settings**

#### Password Management

**Changing Your Password**:
1. Go to Security Settings
2. Click "Change Password"
3. Enter current password
4. Enter new password (must meet requirements)
5. Confirm new password
6. Click "Update Password"

**Password Requirements**:
- Minimum 8 characters
- At least one uppercase letter
- At least one lowercase letter
- At least one number
- At least one special character (!@#$%^&*)
- Cannot be same as last 3 passwords

**Password Best Practices**:
- Use unique password (not used elsewhere)
- Change password every 90 days
- Never share password
- Use password manager for security
- Avoid common words or personal information

#### Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

**What is 2FA?**
An extra security layer that requires both your password AND a code from your phone to log in.

**How to Enable 2FA**:
1. Go to Security Settings
2. Click "Enable Two-Factor Authentication"
3. Choose method:
   - **Authenticator App** (recommended): Google Authenticator, Authy
   - **SMS**: Text message to phone
   - **Email**: Code sent to email
4. Follow setup instructions
5. Save backup codes in secure location

**Using 2FA**:
1. Enter email and password as normal
2. System prompts for verification code
3. Open authenticator app or check SMS/email
4. Enter 6-digit code
5. Click "Verify"
6. Successfully logged in

**Backup Codes**:
- Generated when you enable 2FA
- One-time use codes if you lose phone
- Store securely (password manager or physical safe)
- Can regenerate if needed

**If You Lose Access**:
1. Use backup code to log in
2. Contact support immediately
3. Update 2FA settings
4. Generate new backup codes

#### Session Management

**Managing Sessions**:
- **View All Active Sessions**: See every logged-in device
- **End Individual Session**: Click "End Session" on unfamiliar device
- **End All Other Sessions**: Log out all devices except current
- **Session Timeout**: Automatic logout after 30 minutes of inactivity

**Security Tips**:
- Regularly review active sessions
- End sessions on shared/public computers
- If you see unfamiliar session, end it immediately and change password
- Use "End All Sessions" if you suspect unauthorized access

## 12. Glossary

### Investment Terms

**Accredited Investor**
An individual or entity meeting specific financial thresholds allowing investment in private securities. Requirements include income over $200,000 (individual) or $300,000 (joint) for two years, or net worth exceeding $1 million.

**Asset Allocation**
Distribution of investments across different asset types (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum, USDC) to manage risk and optimize returns.

**AUM (Assets Under Management)**
Total market value of all investments managed by the fund.

**Cost Basis**
Original purchase price of an investment, used to calculate capital gains or losses.

**Fund**
A pooled investment vehicle that combines capital from multiple investors to purchase securities.

**Lock-up Period**
Timeframe during which investors cannot withdraw their funds from an investment.

**NAV (Net Asset Value)**
Per-share value of a fund, calculated by dividing total assets minus liabilities by number of outstanding shares.

**Yield**
Income return on an investment, expressed as a percentage of the investment's cost or current market value.

### Platform-Specific Terms

**Admin Notes**
Messages from platform administrators attached to transactions or withdrawal requests.

**Approval**
Administrative authorization required for certain actions like withdrawals or large transactions.

**KYC (Know Your Customer)**
Verification process to confirm investor identity and assess risk, required by regulations.

**Position**
Your ownership stake in a particular fund, including quantity of shares and current value.

**Profile Status**
Account state: Pending, Active, Suspended, or Closed.

**Statement**
Monthly report showing all account activity, balances, and performance.

**Transaction**
Any movement of funds: deposits, withdrawals, fees, or transfers.

### Cryptocurrency Terms

**Blockchain**
Distributed ledger technology that records all cryptocurrency transactions across a network.

**BTC (Bitcoin)**
The first and most well-known cryptocurrency, often used as a store of value.

**Confirmation**
Verification that a blockchain transaction has been processed and added to the blockchain (multiple confirmations increase security).

**ETH (Ethereum)**
Second-largest cryptocurrency, used for smart contracts and decentralized applications.

**Gas Fees**
Transaction costs on the Ethereum blockchain, paid to miners for processing transactions.

**Network**
The specific blockchain protocol used for a transaction (e.g., Ethereum mainnet, Polygon, Binance Smart Chain).

**Private Key**
Secret code that proves ownership of cryptocurrency; must be kept secure.

**USDC (USD Coin)**
Stablecoin pegged 1:1 to US Dollar, providing price stability.

**Wallet Address**
Unique identifier (string of letters and numbers) used to send and receive cryptocurrency.

### Security Terms

**2FA (Two-Factor Authentication)**
Security method requiring two forms of verification: password + phone code.

**Authenticator App**
Mobile application that generates time-based codes for 2FA (e.g., Google Authenticator, Authy).

**Backup Codes**
One-time use codes for account recovery if you lose 2FA device.

**Biometric Authentication**
Login using fingerprint (Touch ID) or facial recognition (Face ID).

**Encryption**
Process of encoding information so only authorized parties can access it.

**Session**
Period of activity after logging in until logging out or timeout.

**SSL Certificate**
Security protocol ensuring encrypted connection between your browser and the platform.

### Status Indicators

**Active**
Account or position is operational and in good standing.

**Cancelled**
Transaction or request that was terminated before completion.

**Completed**
Transaction fully processed and settled.

**Confirmed**
Transaction verified and finalized.

**Failed**
Transaction attempted but unsuccessful.

**Pending**
Awaiting processing, approval, or confirmation.

**Processing**
Currently being executed.

**Rejected**
Request denied by administrator.
